<p><p>The low carbon methanol market comprises two main types: bio methanol and e-methanol. Bio methanol is produced through the fermentation of biomass or organic waste, leveraging renewable resources to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. In contrast, e-methanol is generated by combining hydrogen derived from renewable electricity with carbon dioxide captured from the atmosphere or industrial processes. Both types play a crucial role in decarbonizing energy sectors and supporting the transition towards sustainable fuels and chemicals.</p></p>
