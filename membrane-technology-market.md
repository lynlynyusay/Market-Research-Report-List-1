<p><p>The membrane technology market encompasses various filtration processes, including ultrafiltration, microfiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration employs membranes to separate larger particles and microorganisms, typically in water treatment and food processing. Ultrafiltration operates on smaller scale, filtering out macromolecules and colloids, commonly used in pharmaceuticals and wastewater treatment. Nanofiltration bridges the gap, removing smaller ions and organic molecules, making it ideal for softening water and concentrating food products. Each type serves distinct applications in diverse industries driven by efficiency and sustainability.</p></p>
