<p><p>The 3D printing market is primarily divided into two types: powder and polymer resins. Powder-based 3D printing utilizes fine powders, often materials like nylon, metal, or ceramics, which are fused together using techniques such as selective laser sintering (SLS). In contrast, polymer resin printing relies on liquid photopolymers that are cured layer by layer with UV light, commonly used in stereolithography (SLA). Both types cater to different applications, offering unique advantages in precision, material properties, and finishing quality.</p></p>
