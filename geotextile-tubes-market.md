<p><p>The geotextile tubes market is categorized into woven and nonwoven types, each serving distinct functions. Woven geotextiles consist of interlaced fibers, offering high tensile strength and drainage capabilities, making them suitable for applications like erosion control and soil stabilization. Nonwoven geotextiles, made from randomly arranged fibers, provide excellent filtration and separation properties, commonly used in environmental and civil engineering projects. Both types play crucial roles in managing soil, water, and structural integrity in various construction and coastal protection efforts.</p></p>
