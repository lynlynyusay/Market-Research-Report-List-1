<p><p>The Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) drug market includes three primary types: H2 receptor antagonists, proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), and antacids. H2 receptor antagonists work by reducing stomach acid production, providing symptomatic relief. PPIs offer more potent acid suppression by blocking proton pumps in the stomach, effectively healing esophageal damage. Antacids act quickly to neutralize existing stomach acid, alleviating discomfort. Together, these treatments address varying severity levels of GERD, catering to diverse patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
