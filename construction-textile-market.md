<p><p>Construction textiles are specialized fabrics used in the construction industry for applications such as reinforcement, insulation, protection, and architectural design. This growth is driven by increasing demand for sustainable and energy-efficient building materials, as well as the rising adoption of advanced construction techniques. Geotextiles, in particular, are gaining traction due to their role in soil stabilization and erosion control in infrastructure projects. Additionally, the use of lightweight and durable architectural membranes is expanding in modern construction for aesthetic and functional purposes.</p><p>Key trends in the market include the development of smart textiles with embedded sensors for structural health monitoring and the integration of eco-friendly materials to align with green building standards. The Asia-Pacific region is emerging as a significant market due to rapid urbanization and infrastructure development, while North America and Europe are focusing on innovative applications and sustainability. Polyethylene offers lightweight, waterproof properties, often used in protective covers and geomembranes. Polypropylene is valued for its chemical resistance and flexibility, commonly used in geotextiles and erosion control.
