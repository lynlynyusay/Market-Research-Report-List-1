<p><p>The Nitinol spring market consists of three primary types: compression springs, extension springs, and torsion springs. Compression springs are designed to resist axial loads and are commonly used in various applications to absorb shock or provide force. Extension springs, on the other hand, are built to stretch and store energy, typically used in scenarios requiring a pulling force. Torsion springs function by twisting and are utilized in mechanisms needing rotational movement, making each type essential for specific operational requirements in various industries.</p></p>
