<p><p>The body exfoliator market features two primary types of exfoliating agents: physical and chemical. Physical exfoliating agents utilize textured particles, such as sugar or salt, to manually slough off dead skin cells through abrasion. In contrast, chemical exfoliating agents contain substances like alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs) or beta-hydroxy acids (BHAs) that dissolve dead skin cells and promote cell turnover through a chemical reaction. Both methods aim to enhance skin texture and appearance, catering to diverse consumer preferences for skincare.</p></p>
