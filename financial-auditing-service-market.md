<p><p>The financial auditing service market comprises two primary types: external and internal audits. External audits are conducted by independent auditors to provide an unbiased opinion on the financial statements of an organization, ensuring compliance with regulations and enhancing credibility with stakeholders. In contrast, internal audits are performed by an organization's own personnel to assess risk management, internal controls, and operational efficiency. Both types play vital roles in enhancing accountability, improving financial accuracy, and fostering trust among investors and regulators.</p></p>
