<p><ul><li>Subcutaneous Immunotherapy</li><li>Sublingual Immunotherapy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The allergic rhinitis immunotherapies market is primarily divided into two types: subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) and sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT). SCIT involves administering allergen extracts via injections, gradually increasing dosage to build tolerance over time. This method is typically conducted in a clinical setting. In contrast, SLIT involves placing allergen tablets or drops under the tongue, allowing for self-administration at home, which enhances convenience. Both therapies aim to reduce symptoms and long-term sensitivity to allergens, offering effective treatment options for patients.</p></p>
